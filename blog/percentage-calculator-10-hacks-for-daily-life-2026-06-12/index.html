Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the difference between percentage and ratio?

A: Percentage is a fraction of 100, while ratio is a comparison of two or more numbers. For example, 25% is the same as 1/4 or 0.25, while 3:2 is a ratio of three to two.

Q: How do I calculate the percentage increase or decrease?

A: To calculate the percentage increase or decrease, use the formula: (New Value - Old Value) / Old Value x 100. For example, if the old value is ₹10,000 and the new value is ₹12,000, the percentage increase is (12,000 - 10,000) / 10,000 x 100 = 20%.

Q: What is the formula for calculating the present value and future value?

A: The formula for calculating the present value is: PV = FV / (1 + r)^n, where PV is the present value, FV is the future value, r is the interest rate, and n is the number of periods. The formula for calculating the future value is: FV = PV x (1 + r)^n.
