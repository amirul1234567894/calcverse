How to Calculate Percentages

A percentage is a number expressed as a fraction of 100. The word comes from the Latin "per centum" meaning "by the hundred." Percentages are used everywhere — from exam scores and sales discounts to tax rates and investment returns.

Basic Percentage Formula

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Common Percentage Calculations

  • X% of Y: (X/100) × Y — e.g., 15% of 200 = 30
  • Percentage change: ((New − Old) / |Old|) × 100
  • Percentage increase: ((New − Old) / Old) × 100 when New > Old
  • Reverse percentage: If Y is X% of Z, then Z = Y / (X/100)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a percentage of a number?
Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 20% of 150 = (20/100) × 150 = 30.
How do I find what percentage one number is of another?
Divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100. For example, 30 is what percentage of 150? (30/150) × 100 = 20%.
