How to Calculate Percentages: All Formulas

Percentage calculations fall into several categories. Here's a complete reference:

Basic Percentage Formula

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Example: If you scored 425 marks out of 500, your percentage = (425/500) × 100 = 85%

Finding X% of a Number

Result = (Percentage × Number) / 100

Example: 25% of 800 = (25 × 800) / 100 = 200

Useful for discount calculations, tip estimations, tax computations, and commission calculations.

Percentage Increase

% Increase = [(New − Old) / Old] × 100

Example: Salary increased from ₹50,000 to ₹60,000. Percentage increase = [(60,000 - 50,000) / 50,000] × 100 = 20% hike

Percentage Decrease

% Decrease = [(Old − New) / Old] × 100

Example: Phone price dropped from ₹30,000 to ₹24,000. Percentage decrease = [(30,000 - 24,000) / 30,000] × 100 = 20% discount

Reverse Percentage (Finding the Whole)

Whole = (Part × 100) / Percentage

Example: If 15% of a number is 75, what is the number? Number = (75 × 100) / 15 = 500

Percentage in Business and Finance

Percentages drive critical business metrics:

  • Profit Margin: (Profit / Revenue) × 100 — Indicates business efficiency
  • ROI (Return on Investment): (Gain / Cost) × 100 — Measures investment performance
  • Market Share: (Your Sales / Total Market) × 100 — Shows competitive position
  • Conversion Rate: (Customers / Visitors) × 100 — E-commerce key metric
  • Customer Retention: (Returning Customers / Total) × 100 — Loyalty indicator
  • Tax Rate: (Tax Paid / Income) × 100 — Effective tax burden
  • Inflation Rate: [(New CPI - Old CPI) / Old CPI] × 100 — Economic indicator

Common Percentage Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing increase and decrease: A 50% increase followed by 50% decrease does NOT return to original. ₹100 → ₹150 → ₹75 (25% net loss)
  • Adding percentages of different bases: 10% of A + 10% of B ≠ 10% of (A+B). Calculate each separately.
  • Compounding errors: Two 10% raises don't equal 20%. They equal 21% due to compounding.
  • Reverse calculation errors: If item is priced ₹1,200 after 20% discount, original is ₹1,500, not ₹1,440 (working backwards needs different formula).
  • Percentage points vs percentage: Interest rate increase from 5% to 6% is "1 percentage point" or "20% increase" — very different!
  • Forgetting to multiply by 100: Decimal result of 0.25 is 25%, not 0.25%.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a percentage of a number?
Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 20% of 150 = (20/100) × 150 = 30.
How do I find what percentage one number is of another?
Divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100. For example, 30 is what percentage of 150? (30/150) × 100 = 20%.
